India is a country in South Asia. It is the seventh-largest country by area and the second-most populous country in the world. India shares borders with Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.

The Prime Minister of India is the head of government of the Republic of India. The Prime Minister is appointed by the President of India and is usually the leader of the party or coalition that has a majority in the Lok Sabha.

New Delhi is the capital of India. It is located in northern India and serves as the seat of all three branches of the Government of India. New Delhi is part of the National Capital Territory of Delhi.

The Indian economy is one of the fastest-growing major economies in the world. India has a mixed economy with agriculture, manufacturing, and services sectors. The service sector contributes the largest share to India's GDP.

Hindi is one of the official languages of India, along with English. Hindi is spoken by a large portion of the Indian population, especially in the northern and central regions of the country.

The Indian flag has three horizontal stripes of equal width. The top stripe is saffron, the middle stripe is white with a navy blue Ashoka Chakra in the center, and the bottom stripe is green.

India gained independence from British rule on August 15, 1947. This day is celebrated as Independence Day every year. The struggle for independence was led by Mahatma Gandhi and other freedom fighters.

The Indian Parliament consists of two houses: the Lok Sabha (House of the People) and the Rajya Sabha (Council of States). The Lok Sabha is the lower house and is directly elected by the people.

Cricket is the most popular sport in India. The Indian national cricket team has won multiple international tournaments including the Cricket World Cup. Many Indian cricketers are celebrated as national heroes.

The Ganges is one of the major rivers in India. It flows through northern India and is considered sacred by Hindus. The river originates in the Himalayas and flows into the Bay of Bengal.

The Indian Constitution is the supreme law of India. It was adopted on January 26, 1950, which is celebrated as Republic Day. The Constitution establishes India as a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic.

The President of India is the head of state and the commander-in-chief of the Indian Armed Forces. The President is elected by an electoral college and serves a term of five years.

Mumbai is the financial capital of India. It is the most populous city in India and is home to the Bombay Stock Exchange, one of the largest stock exchanges in the world.

The Taj Mahal is one of the most famous monuments in India. It is located in Agra and was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is considered one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Yoga originated in ancient India and is now practiced worldwide. The International Day of Yoga is celebrated on June 21 every year. Yoga combines physical, mental, and spiritual practices.

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of India. ISRO has successfully launched numerous satellites and missions, including the Mars Orbiter Mission, making India the first Asian nation to reach Mars orbit.
